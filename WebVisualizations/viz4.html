How do city latitude and wind speed affect the aurora borealis?

This is a scatterplot analyzing city latitude and wind speed. Wind speed doesn't actually affect the aurora borealis. Solar winds do, but not winds in the area where you'd be standing. However, wind speed is useful for cloudiness! If it's cloudy, a windy night can blow away the clouds and make the aurora borealis easier to see. This graph shows that there is a chance for higher wind speeds at cities 60 degrees latitude or higher, so that's good news for us since we're going to be going to one of these cities.
