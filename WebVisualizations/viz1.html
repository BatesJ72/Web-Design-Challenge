How do latitude and temperature affect the aurora borealis?

This is a scatterplot analyzing the latitude of cities vs the temperature. As you can see, there is a correlation for cities with a higher latitude having lower temperatures. Interestingly, temperature doesn't affect the aurora borealis! People often think that colder weather means the aurora borealis is more active, but actually the correlation is that when it's colder - i.e. winter months - there are more hours of night time and thus more available hours to see the aurora borealis.
